Description

This project is being undertaken as part of Masters Research study for Jessie Roberts.   The purpose of this project is evaluate effectiveness of different methods of presenting statistical information to non-expert audiences.   Any members of the general public that are over 18 yrs of age are invited to participate in this research.

Participation

Your participation will involve playing an online game. In the game you are a pirate of the sea and will be asked to allocate gold bullion, for resources and ammunition, in order to maximize your chance of winning against another ship.  

 

Your personal information is not recorded in the process of participating in this game. At the end of the game you will be asked some additional information, this information is not identifiable in any way. Your participation in this game is entirely voluntary. If you do agree to participate you can withdraw from the game without comment or penalty.

 

Expected benefits

This research will also contribute to the wider field of science communication by furthering our understanding of how to effectively visualise and communicate the reliability and uncertainty of scientific information to non-expert decision-makers.

 

Risks

We do not anticipate this game to trigger any emotional stress and does not represent any physical risks to participants.

 

PRIVACY AND Confidentiality

The names of individual persons are not required to participate in this game and no identifiable information will be collected. All data collected as part of this project will be stored securely as per QUT’s Management of research data policy.

The project is funded by the, Cancer Council Queensland, QUT IMPACT Philanthropy,  and the Australian Institute of Health and Well-Being (AIHW). QUT the Cancer Council QLD and AIHW will have access to the data obtained during the project. No data gathered throughout the game will contain any personal identifying information.

 

Please note that non-identifiable data extracted from the game may be used as comparative data in future projects or stored on an open access database for secondary analysis.
